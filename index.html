About Me

Every great life begins with an even greater story.

Hello, I'm Scott and you have reached my official website!


Welcome!


I bet you one question alone led you here, "Who is Scott?", and as it happens, this is where the answer lies! Please, grab your favorite beer from that ice bucket over there, take a seat and let's get to know each other.


I'm the only child my parents have and I grew up in a military household down in Monkeybay, my father was a marine. These two facts played a role in the formation of my personality. I'm a total introvert.


Oh and, why you holding an empty bottle, feel free to grab another. Life is short, you have to enjoy every moment!


I was introduced to morden tech by a friend I had back in 2014, when I was graced with a chance to walk the corridors of UNIMA, a walk that unfortunately lasted only 12 months.


Samuel Yute was his name. He introduced me to Web Development, gave me tutorials on HTML, CSS and JavaScript created by a guy named Bucky Roberts. This was a significant moment in my life, and I never forget it, even the smallest of details.


During my college days, I kept deeping my feet in the software development industry just enough to create a buzz among my friends as the genius coder, but not enough to actually start and complete a project by myself.


Still, I loved the tech industry so much, but I just didn't know what to focus on, or what to do with the knowledge I was acquiring. Therefore, my career, together with my general life, drifted through time and space aimlessly and a lot happened.


Fast forward to this moment, I have worked 3 jobs, resigned at all of them, in pursuit of a different lifestyle. Looking to go against the status quo, on the road less traveled, until i settled on technical writing, with web development as a fun hobby.


Additionally, Astrophysics and Cosmology are my other passions. The vastness of the universe, the mysteries of black holes, and the wonders of distant galaxies captivate my imagination. I find solace in contemplating the cosmos and our place within it. I am always fascinated when listening to the likes of Neil deGrasse Tyson, Micchio Kaku, Brian Cox, Brian Green, etc.
